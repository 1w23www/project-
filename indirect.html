Indirect cyberbullying

What is it?

Indirect cyberbullying is the repeated, negative use of actions, which are neither physical nor verbal, to intentionally hurt others online cyberbullying by proxy is using others to help cyberbully, either with or without the accomplice's knowledge.

catfishing

How to Deal with Cyberbullying catfishing:

  • Educate yourself and others about the signs of catfishing.
  • Verify the identities of online friends and acquaintances.
  • Keep personal information private and limit what you share online.
  • Use online search tools to check the authenticity of images or profiles.
  • Never send money to someone you have only met online.
  • Be cautious when someone you meet online seems too good to be true.
  • Report suspected catfishers to social media platforms and authorities.
  • Seek support from friends, family, or professionals if you're targeted.

hacking

How to Deal with Cyberbullying hacking:

  • Use complex passwords and enable two-factor authentication for all online accounts.
  • Stay vigilant against phishing attempts and do not click on suspicious links.
  • Keep software and antivirus programs up to date to protect against vulnerabilities.
  • Use secure Wi-Fi networks and consider a VPN for public internet access.
  • Regularly monitor account activity logs for signs of unauthorized access.
  • Back up important data to recover it easily in case of unauthorized access.
  • Adjust privacy settings to limit the public sharing of personal information.
  • Support victims and promote awareness of the effects and illegality of cyberbullying.

peers

How to Deal with peer Cyberbullying :

  • Encourage open dialogue about cyberbullying in schools and communities.
  • Implement and enforce clear anti-cyberbullying policies in educational institutions.
  • Conduct workshops and training sessions on digital citizenship and empathy.
  • Create a safe space for victims to report cyberbullying incidents.
  • Promote peer mentoring programs to foster a supportive school environment.
  • Involve parents in awareness programs to monitor and guide online behavior at home.
  • Utilize counseling services to provide support for both victims and perpetrators.
  • Collaborate with tech companies to develop tools that help detect and report bullying behavior.
