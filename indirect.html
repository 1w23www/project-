Indirect cyberbullying

What is it?

Indirect cyberbullying is the repeated, negative use of actions, which are neither physical nor verbal, to intentionally hurt others online cyberbullying by proxy is using others to help cyberbully, either with or without the accomplice's knowledge.



Examples

Catfishing

The bully creates a fake online identity to befriend the victim, gather personal information, and then use that information to encourage others to harass the victim.

Hacking or Impersonation:

The cyberbully could hack into the victimâ€™s social media or email accounts and send harmful messages to others in the victim's name, damaging their reputation and relationships.

Manipulating Friends or Peer:.

The cyberbully convinces or tricks friends or peers into cyberbullying the victim. They might spread false information or rumors to incite others to harass the victim.

How to Deal with Cyberbullying catfishing:

  1. Avoid responding with aggression or engaging in a confrontation. Responding calmly can reduce the likelihood of escalation.
  2. Block the harasser and report the incident to the relevant platform or website. Most platforms have mechanisms for reporting harassment.
    3. Document Evidence:
  3. Keep records of all harassing messages or activities. This documentation can be helpful when reporting the incidenfot or seeking assistance.
  4. Talk to friends, family, or colleagues about the situation. Emotional support is crucial in dealing with the impact of harassment.
  5. If the harassment involves serious threats or illegal activities, consider reporting it to law enforcement.
  6. Adjust privacy settings on your social media accounts to control who can see your information and interactions.
  7. Stay informed about online safety, privacy settings, and the features provided by the platforms you use.
  8. If the harassment is causing severe emotional distress, consider seeking help from mental health professionals or counseling services.
  9. Advocate for positive online behavior and support initiatives that promote a safe and inclusive online environment.
    10. Remember, each situation is unique, and the appropriate response may vary. If you or someone you know is experiencing cyberbullying harassment, it's essential to take the necessary steps to address the issue promptly and seek support. Always prioritize your safety and well-being.
