Direct Cyberbullying

What is it?

Direct cyberbullying refers to the act of using digital communication tools or online platforms to intentionally harass, threaten, or intimidate another individual.

Examples

Harassment

Repeated and unwanted messages, comments, or online actions intended to disturb or upset the targeted individual.




Impersonation

Creating fake profiles or using someone else's identity to spread false information or engage in harmful behavior.

Flaming

Engaging in hostile and aggressive online discussions or arguments with the intention of causing emotional distress.
